EV Sales Revenue Optimisation Analysis Using Retail Sales Data
Project Overview
This project analyses sales data from an electric vehicle (EV) retailer to identify the key factors influencing sales revenue. The analysis focuses on product performance, marketing channels, promotions, customer segments, and sales representative attributes during the most recent quarter.

The project applies data-driven analysis using R to generate actionable insights that support revenue optimisation and strategic planning.
________________________________________
Research Objective

•	Identify which EV products generate the highest revenue

•	Examine the effectiveness of marketing channels and promotions

•	Analyse customer segments contributing most to revenue

•	Assess how sales representative experience, qualification, and incentives impact sales

•	Generate practical recommendations to improve revenue performance

________________________________________
Dataset

•	Source: Internal EV product and sales database

•	Time Period: Last 12 months (analysis focused on most recent quarter)

•	Observations: 42,125

•	Variables: 13 after cleaning and transformation

•	Outcome Variable: Purchase value (£)
________________________________________
Methodology

•	Data cleaning and preparation

•	Feature engineering

•	Exploratory data analysis

•	Revenue distribution analysis

•	Group comparisons across products, customers, and channels

•	Correlation analysis between incentives, marketing spend, and sales

•	Business-focused interpretation of results

________________________________________
Key Findings

•	Sales revenue follows a normal distribution, indicating stable performance

•	Sedan models are the highest revenue contributors

•	Promotions significantly increase sales compared to regular pricing

•	TikTok is the most effective marketing channel

•	Single customers contribute the largest share of revenue

•	Higher commission incentives are associated with higher sales

•	Sales representatives with more experience and higher qualifications outperform others

•	Marketing spend shows diminishing returns, suggesting inefficient allocation

Practical Implications

•	Reallocating marketing resources to high-performing channels

•	Optimising promotional strategies

•	Introducing targeted incentive schemes for sales representatives

•	Prioritising experienced and highly qualified sales staff
